4 questions to answer before you adopt a pet during the holidays

By Heather Stone | November 27, 2022

The holidays are an exciting time and it is completely understandable wanting to expand your family with the addition of a furry friend. Who wouldn’t want to wake up to a new pet under the tree? Unfortunately, this excitement can sometimes lead to poor planning when adopting.
Set your home up for success and consider these 4 questions before adopting this holiday season!

  • 1

    Do you have the time?

  • 2

    Have you considered the costs?

  • 3

    Is the pet a good fit?

  • 4

    Is now the right time?

1

Do you have the time?

Looking after an animal takes some effort and this is something that gets surprisingly overlooked in the midst of excitement.

Part of adopting a pet is the commitment of continued love and care even after the newness has worn off. Maybe your pet is taking a little longer to adjust to their new home, maybe you’re finding that they require daily long walks and consistent training or maybe they are pretty low maintenance and just want occasional attention from you. Whatever it may be, the journey is in learning their needs and welcoming them into your daily life.

Make sure you are willing to dedicate this time to your new pet so you can give them the life that they deserve.

2

Have you considered the costs?

There is more to owning an animal than just cuddles - if only! In addition to providing food and shelter, there are other regular costs and potential costs of pet care.

A few cost to consider with animal adoption:

  • Routine checkups

  • Emergency care

  • Extra care associated with bread or genetics

  • Travel arrangements

  • Accessories, toys and extra curricular activities

Think about these costs ahead of time to determine if you can make the financial commitment. This gives you the opportunity to plan ahead and budget accordingly so you are prepared.

3

Is the pet a good fit?

As soon as the idea of adopting a pet comes to mind it is only natural to picture what that might look like. Is it coming home to your cat after a typical long day at work and relaxing on the couch together? Is it heading out for your daily jog with your dog? Perhaps it’s bringing your pet along on family hikes, beach days or road trips.

Think about how a pet might fit into your lifestyle. Do you have a children? Do you like to get outdoors? Are you more of a homebody? Then think about what type of animal will be the most compatible. Think about pet type and breed, size and age, personality and temperament.
Most shelters clearly define their adoption process and policies and that is to ensure you are matched with a pet that is compatible with your lifestyle.

4

Is now the right time?

Between traveling and hosting friends or family, the holidays tend to be a busy time. On one hand, you might have the extra time off to devote to helping your new pet adjust. On the other hand, if you have trips planned or tend to frequently host holiday get-togethers, now might not be the best time to add a new pet to the mix.

Adopting a pet can be quite stressful for the animal as they are placed in a new environment. You want to make sure you have time set aside to help them adjust to your family and home.

Key takeaways

The holidays can be a great time to adopt a pet but no matter the time of year, careful consideration is and a little planning is key! Think about whether or not you have the time for a new pet, if you can afford the costs, what type of pet would be most compatible with your lifestyle and whether or not adopting during the holidays is the best time based on your schedule.
